User's guide and credits

Web Development Stack: HTML, CSS & Google Fonts, JS & AJAX, Python & Flask & Jinja

Welcome to the website, if it's your first time. I just wanted to give a quick helping guide for those that could struggle in understanding. If the symbols are unfamiliar to you, hover over them to get the text format. Have a quick read of the home page it gives a very brief summary. To become a user, you'd need to login securely with correct email address and password (and had to register beforehand, which you already know). Your email address and username will need to be unique (if you're here they must be).

Next let's have a talk on the top panel. Home page will lead you to our welcome page. The bell icon is for notifications. You'd get notifications, only when there's a new bill payment, and you'd get a small red number for number of unread notifications (straightforward right). To logout it's the far right symbol on the dashboard. And obviously you know now where the guide is and are free to refer to it when needed.

The final and main part (you might want to skip here for referring quickly) is the dashboard panel. To create a bill which will automatically be saved as a draft, click the filter icon, and click the purple button. Quick note whenever you create a bill or pay a share of a bill, you need to show payment proof (in the form of an image). Also you need to select one or more users (and the money will be distributed evenly or as evenly as you can get when amount isn't divisible by number of bill payers for the bill). Once you've submitted a created paid bill with necessary fields filled, then it gets automatically shown on the first table (paid bill drafts), there are two actions to send (in which case you'd lose edit access and users can now pay the bill you've proposed), or you can edit (if there are incorrect details you can change them). Once sent, your bill record is moved to the second table (your paid bills) and users who are meant to pay you a bill share, will receive a notification as well as the bill share details in the third table (your owed bills). Then they'd have the option to reject your payment in the third table, if they feel like an unknown person has sent a payment, and it will be sent back to your drafts. If you owed someone a bill then it'd end up in the third table as well.

Final tips: To view all shared users and their payee receipts do click on > View...; it's hidden in case the list of shared users and so their payee receipts are too long. Please ensure as the creator of the paid bill, that the due date isn't the deadline of when the bill's actually paid (this isn't a bill tracker). Due date in this website and context means when all shares of a bill have to be paid back to the person who paid the bill in full for all users. Statuses in Shared Users & Statuses and Statuses just shows what their/a current bill share payment status is e.g. Overdue (deadline's passed and user hasn't paid debt back), Late Payment, Due, Paid on Time. Amount shown in the third table (your owed bills) is the actual share amount to pay the creator of the paid bill, not the full amount of the paid bill.
